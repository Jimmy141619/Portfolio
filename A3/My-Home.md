# A3: Security Concepts at Home

## **Overview**

This analysis examines security mechanisms used within my residential home environment. Home security relies on a combination of physical barriers, access control, and smart technologies to protect occupants and assets.

---

## Security Measures Identified

### Door Locks (Physical Access Control)

* Standard locks are installed on entry doors.

**Purpose:**

* Prevent unauthorised entry
* Provide a basic layer of physical security

**Weaknesses:**

* Can be picked, bypassed, or forced
* Effectiveness depends on lock quality

---

### Garage Door (Controlled Entry Point)

* The garage door acts as an additional entry point to the house.

**Purpose:**

* Secures vehicles and provides controlled access to the home
* Acts as a secondary barrier

**Weaknesses:**

* Can be exploited if left open or poorly secured
* Remote systems may be vulnerable to signal interception

---

### Roller Shutters (Physical Barrier)

* External shutters are used on windows or doors.

**Purpose:**

* Provides an additional physical barrier against intrusion
* Deters break-ins by increasing effort required

**Weaknesses:**

* Can still be forced with sufficient tools
* May create a false sense of security

---

### Lockbox for Keys (Key Management)

* A lockbox is used to store spare keys.

**Purpose:**

* Allows controlled access without distributing physical keys
* Useful for trusted individuals

**Weaknesses:**

* If the code is known or guessed, access is compromised
* Physical tampering may be possible

---

### Smart Home Device (Google Home)

* A smart assistant device is used within the home.

**Purpose:**

* Enables voice control and automation of devices
* Can integrate with security systems

**Weaknesses:**

* Always-on listening introduces privacy concerns
* Vulnerable to hacking or unauthorized access if poorly secured

###

---

## Risk Analysis

Despite multiple security measures, several risks remain:

* Weak physical locks may be bypassed
* Smart devices introduce cyber and privacy risks
* Poor key management (lockbox misuse) may lead to unauthorized access
* Accessible tools may assist intruders

---

## Recommendations

* Upgrade to high-security locks where possible
* Secure smart devices with strong passwords and updates
* Limit visibility and access to lockboxes
* Store tools in secure locations

---

## Conclusion

Home security relies on a combination of physical and digital controls. While effective at a basic level, weaknesses in implementation and user behaviour can significantly impact overall security.
