# A10: Discover Privacy Techniques Used Offline

## **Overview**

This activity examines privacy techniques used offline to protect personal information and reduce the risk of unauthorized access. Offline privacy focuses on physical environments and how information is handled outside of digital networks.

---

## Privacy Techniques

### 1. Secure Storage Using a Safe

* Sensitive documents (e.g., passports, financial records) are stored in a safe.

**Purpose:**

* Protects personal information from unauthorized access
* Reduces risk of theft or misuse of sensitive documents

**Weaknesses:**

* Safes can still be forced open depending on quality
* Security depends on proper use and placement

---

### 2. Limiting Physical Access to Devices and Items

* Devices and important items are not left unattended and are kept in controlled locations.

**Purpose:**

* Prevents unauthorized access to personal data
* Reduces risk of theft or misuse

**Weaknesses:**

* Relies heavily on user behaviour
* Does not prevent access by trusted individuals

---

## Conclusion

Offline privacy techniques such as secure storage and limiting physical access are essential for protecting personal information. While simple, these methods are effective when consistently applied and combined with other security measures.
