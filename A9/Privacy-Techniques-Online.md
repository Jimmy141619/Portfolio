# A9: Discover Privacy Techniques Used Online

## **Overview**

This activity examines privacy techniques used online to protect user data and reduce tracking. These techniques help limit how personal information is collected, stored, and shared across online platforms.

---

## Privacy Techniques

### 1. Virtual Private Network (VPN)

* Encrypts internet traffic and hides the user’s IP address.

**Purpose:**

* Protects privacy on public networks
* Prevents websites and third parties from identifying user location

**Weaknesses:**

* Requires trust in the VPN provider
* May reduce internet speed

---

### 2. Browser Privacy Controls (Blocking Trackers & Cookies)

* Modern browsers allow users to block third-party cookies and trackers.

**Purpose:**

* Reduces tracking across websites
* Limits data collection by advertisers

**Weaknesses:**

* Some websites may break or lose functionality
* Does not eliminate all forms of tracking

---

### 3. Incognito / Private Browsing Mode

* Prevents the browser from storing history, cookies, and session data locally.

**Purpose:**

* Protects privacy on shared devices
* Reduces stored personal data on the device

**Weaknesses:**

* Does not hide activity from websites, ISPs, or networks
* Often misunderstood as complete anonymity

---

## Conclusion

Online privacy techniques help reduce data exposure and tracking but do not guarantee complete anonymity. Effective privacy requires combining multiple techniques and understanding their limitations.
