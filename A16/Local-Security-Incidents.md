# A16: Discover 3 Local Security Incidents (Western Australia)

## **Overview**

This activity examines three cybersecurity incidents relevant to Western Australia. These incidents demonstrate the impact of cyberattacks on local organisations, universities, and businesses, highlighting risks to data security and critical operations.

---

## Security Incidents

### 1. University of Western Australia (UWA) Data Breach


**Summary:**

* The University of Western Australia experienced a major data breach affecting its systems.
* Sensitive student and staff information was potentially exposed.

**Security Impact:**

* Exposure of personal and academic data
* Risk of identity theft and phishing attacks

**Key Insight:**

* Educational institutions are high-value targets due to large volumes of personal data and often weaker security controls.

---

### 2. WA-Based Company Targeted by $30 Million Ransomware Attack

**Summary:**

* A major company with operations in Western Australia was targeted by a ransomware attack demanding approximately $30 million.
* Attackers attempted to encrypt systems and disrupt business operations.

**Security Impact:**

* Significant operational disruption
* Financial loss and extortion pressure
* Potential exposure of sensitive corporate data

**Key Insight:**

* Ransomware attacks are increasingly targeting large organisations, using financial pressure to force compliance.

---

### 3. Latitude Financial Data Breach (Australia, Impacting WA Residents)

**Summary:**

* Latitude Financial suffered a major cyberattack impacting over 14 million customers across Australia and New Zealand.

* The breach was caused by stolen employee credentials, which allowed attackers to access sensitive customer data.

* Exposed data included:

  * Full names
  * Addresses
  * Phone numbers
  * Dates of birth
  * Driver’s licence and passport details

**Security Impact:**

* Large-scale exposure of sensitive personal information
* Increased risk of identity theft and fraud
* Legal and regulatory consequences for the company

**Key Insight:**

* Weak credential security and excessive data retention significantly increase the impact of a breach.

---

## Conclusion

These local incidents demonstrate that cyber threats affect a wide range of sectors, including education, business, and financial services. The attacks highlight the importance of strong access control, data protection, and incident response strategies to reduce risk and impact.
